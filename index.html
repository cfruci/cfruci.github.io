About

I live in Denver with my beautiful wife and two sons. We spend a lot of time outside in our backyard and on walks around the neighborhood. We laugh a lot. We're joyful and blessed.

I've had a winding, yet fulfilling career. After graduating from Vanderbilt in 2013, I moved to Colorado with a few of my closest friends. We played progressive rock music together in a band called Archive Nights.

Our Clock Ocean - EP holds a special place in my heart. Check it out here. Every once in awhile, I'll listen to this album and get emotional. Songs like "Why I" and "Clock Ocean" feel like they were gifted to us.

Around the time Archive Nights broke up, I joined the DaVita "Village" in 2015 as a corporate strategy analyst. Our team was full of thoughtful, intelligent, and kind people. I grew a lot personally and professionally. And we had a lot of fun.

I left DaVita in 2018 to give music another shot. My bandmate and I wanted to give more time and energy to an exciting new project, Compass + Cavern. Over the next 7 years, we took risks and became better performers. We learned how to run a business and make a creative endeavor profitable. I'm so proud of what we accomplished.

We fought hard through COVID, got married to our spouses, and became parents. In 2025, shortly after releasing our full-length album, Shift, we decided to go on an indefinite hiatus.

We're still performing together in a rock Disney cover band called The Man Cubs with three other close friends. It's a thrill.

While building Compass + Cavern, I started copywriting on a freelance basis. Within two years, I was supporting 10-12 businesses at a time and providing a wide range of content and growth marketing services. I started studying the art of persuasion and the craft of writing more closely.
